06

How this deal could lose money

Every assumption below has a failure mode. Here is each one, quantified, with what protects against it. Capital is returned after selling roughly 95 of 141 homes — houses 96 through 141 are the profit.

Absorption stalls

Plan needs 2.35 homes/month. The market absorbs 58–65/month — the plan requires under 4% market share. The model stays profitable down to 1.8/month.

Mitigant: international broker network (Sotheby's, Engel & Völkers), presales open before construction of homes begins.

Construction costs overrun

Materials inflation in the Riviera Maya runs ~5% annually. A sustained spike compresses margin.

Mitigant: turnkey contracts with EGD Construcciones (BIM-managed), 1% contingency reserve, 98% locally sourced materials.

Permits delay

Coastal Quintana Roo carries real environmental scrutiny (SEMARNAT). A rejected MIA stops the project.

Mitigant: land-use certificate granted January 2021; MIA filed (ordinary modality); density 21% below the permitted ceiling; 75.8% of land preserved green.

Peso/dollar swings

Investment and sales are denominated in MXN. A strong peso reduces USD-equivalent returns for dollar-based investors.

Mitigant: costs are 98% peso-denominated (natural hedge); 65% of Riviera Maya buyers pay in USD terms — a weak peso accelerates their purchases.

Tourism cycle turns

A demand shock (recession, pandemic-type event) cuts buyer flow and absorption simultaneously.

Mitigant: buyer base spans five nationalities; tourism recovered from 7.5M (2020) to 20.8M (2025); cash position stays positive every single month in the model — no forced selling.

Oversupply

The Riviera Maya pipeline is heavy — 74+ active developments in Playa del Carmen alone.

Mitigant: 90%+ of new inventory is vertical (condos). Single-family homes of 250 m² are under 5% of new supply — this is the underserved niche.
